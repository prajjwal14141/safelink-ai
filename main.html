A 'malicious link' refers to a hyperlink that is intentionally designed to redirect users to a harmful website controlled by an attacker. It is a URL that, when accessed, directs the person to a website or web page designed by criminals to cause damage to devices or steal information. These links are often used in phishing attacks, where criminals try to trick their victims into providing data, such as passwords, financial information, or personal details.

These links can come through a variety of channels, such as:

  • Email
  • SMS
  • Social media
  • Chat apps

In this way, victims are taken to fake websites that pose as legitimate, such as banks, online stores, or services. Their goal is to trick people into giving up their information.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can you identify a suspicious URL?

It’s very difficult to determine if a website will be dangerous just by looking at the URL, especially if it’s hidden under a URL shortener. You can, however, look for typos or changed letters if it’s a well-known domain. For example, if you see arnazon.com, it’s a dead giveaway that someone is trying to scam you with a fake Amazon website.

If you want to be sure, use a free URL checker like Link Checker or a paid services that offers this feature.

What happens if you click on a malicious link?

There’s a chance that nothing will happen — you will immediately see that it’s a suspicious website and leave. But it could also lead to data theft (phishing campaigns are designed to steal credit card info, login details, and other personal information) or malware installed on your device. A drive-by download could inject your device with malware that encrypts your data, showers you with ads, or steals your passwords.
